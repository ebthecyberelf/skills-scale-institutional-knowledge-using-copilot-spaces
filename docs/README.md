# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

OctoAcme uses a lightweight, structured lifecycle that moves projects through initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective improvement. Teams begin with a project one-pager to define the problem, goals, success metrics, stakeholders, timeline, and initial risks, then use that foundation to make a go/no-go decision before detailed planning.

During planning and execution, teams break work into shippable increments with clear acceptance criteria, estimates, owners, and a shared Definition of Done. Delivery is tracked on a project board and reinforced by regular standups, weekly delivery syncs, and sprint or milestone demos so progress, blockers, and dependencies are visible and actionable.

Roles and personas provide clear accountability across the workflow. Project Managers coordinate delivery, risk, and communication; Product Managers define outcomes and priorities; Developers implement and test changes; QA validates acceptance criteria and quality; and stakeholders provide guidance and approvals throughout the project lifecycle.

Communication, risk management, and quality assurance are embedded in day-to-day work rather than treated as end-stage activities. Teams maintain a risk register, follow escalation paths from team triage through sponsor-level escalation when needed, and rely on CI testing, security scanning, smoke testing, release checklists, rollback planning, and retrospectives with owned action items to continuously improve delivery.
